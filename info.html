Proxima Centauri b
The closet known planet exoplanet to the Solar System and beleived to be a Earth-like planet. Read More

i) Within the habitable zone of the red dwarf star Proxima Centauri.

ii) Part of the larger triple star system Alpha Centauri.

iii) 4.2 light-years (1.3 parsecs) from Earth.

iv) Orbits its parent star at a distance of about 0.04856 AU.

v) Orbital Period: 11.2 Earth days.

vi) Minimum mass of at least 1.07 times Mass of Earth.

vii) Mean radius = (0.94–1.4) times Radius of Earth.

viii) Proxima Centauri b is unlikely to have any obliquity.

ix) Likely to be tidally locked.

x) Receives about 65% of Earth's (ir)radiation.

Kepler-186f
The first planet with a radius similar to Earth. Read more

i) Earth-sized exoplanet.

ii) Host Star: Red dwarf star Kepler-186.

iii) 580 light-years from Earth.

iv) Orbits its star at a distance of about 0.43 AU.

v) Orbital period: Roughly 130 days.

vi) Mass and radius around 1.44 and 1.17 times that of Earth.

vii) The first planet with a radius similar to Earth's to be discovered in the habitable zone of another star.

viii) Could be a rocky terrestrial planet or a lower density ocean planet with a thick atmosphere.

ix) Estimated equilibrium temperature for Kepler-186f, which is the surface temperature without an atmosphere, is said to be around 188 K.

i) Habitable zone of the red dwarf LHS 1140.

iii) Mean radius (1.730±0.025) times the radius of Earth.

GJ 357d

i) "Super-Earth" within the circumstellar habitable zone of its parent star.

ii) 31 light-years from the Solar System.

iii) Mass = (6.1±1.0) times the mass of Earth.

K2 72e
Most probably tidely locked. Read more

i) Orbiting within the habitable zone of the red dwarf star K2-72.

ii) About 217.1 light-years (66.56 parsecs) from Earth.

iii) Mean radius = (1.29 +0.14) times the radius of Earth.

iv) Mass ~2.21 times the mass of Earth.

v) Orbital period of 24 days and an orbital radius of about 0.1 times that of Earth's.

vi) The exoplanet, along with K2-72c, was announced to be orbiting in the habitable zone of its parent star, the region where, with the correct conditions and atmospheric properties, liquid water may exist on the surface of the planet.

TRAPPIST-1d
TRAPPIST-1d orbits the ultracool dwarf star TRAPPIST-1. Read more

i) Host Star: ultra-cool dwarf star TRAPPIST-1.

ii) 40 light-years away from Earth.

iii) Smaller and less massive than Earth, with an estimated radius of about 0.77 times that of Earth and a mass of approximately 0.388 Earth masses.

iv) Very close to its host star, completing one orbit in just about 4.05 Earth days. TRAPPIST-1 is much cooler than our Sun, so the planet receives similar levels of stellar radiation to what Earth receives from the Sun. Orbital radius (or semi-major axis) of approximately 0.021 astronomical units (AU).

v) Located near the inner edge of the system's habitable zone.

vi) Likely tidally locked.

HD 209458b
Gas giant exoplanet, also known as a "hot Jupiter". Read more

i) Host Star: F-type star.

ii) About 150 light-years away in the constellation Pegasus.

iii) Mass: Approximately 0.73 times the mass of Jupiter.

iv) Radius: About 1.39 times the radius of Jupiter.

v) Completes one orbit in about 3.5 days.

vi) Orbital Radius: Approximately 0.04707 AU (astronomical units).

vii) Discovery: Announced in 1999.

viii) Discovery Method: Radial velocity.

ix) Dayside temperature is around 1,226°C (2,239°F), and the nightside temperature is around 699°C (1,290°F).

x) Observations have shown that the atmosphere is evaporating into space, losing hydrogen gas at a rate of at least 10,000 tonnes per second.
